Common EPA 608 Mistakes to Avoid

Dec 5, 2025 · 6 min read

Introduction

EPA 608 certification is crucial for HVAC technicians working with refrigerants. Many candidates make avoidable mistakes.

Understanding EPA 608

Required for technicians who maintain or dispose of appliances containing regulated refrigerants. Four sections: Core, Type I, Type II, and Type III. Passing Core is mandatory.

Underestimating Core

The Core section includes ozone depletion, refrigerant safety, and environmental regulations. Failing Core means no certification at all.

Ignoring Appliance Differences

Mistaking procedures between system types is common. Memorize pressure ranges, recovery requirements, and leak repair thresholds for each type.

Memorizing Without Understanding

Memorizing answers backfires when questions are reworded. Focus on reasoning behind each process.

Not Using Practice Exams

Practice exams identify strengths and weaknesses and prepare you for the format and pacing.

Updates and Time Management

Refrigerant regulations change. Stay updated. Practice pacing with timed quizzes.

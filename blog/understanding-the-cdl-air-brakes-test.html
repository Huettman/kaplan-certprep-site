Understanding the CDL Air Brakes Test

Nov 15, 2025 · 8 min read

Introduction

Understanding the CDL air brake test is essential if you plan to operate vehicles with air brakes. It is a legal requirement for many positions.

What Is the Test?

The test ensures drivers understand air brake systems and can operate them safely. Without passing, you cannot drive vehicles with air brakes.

What It Covers

System Components: compressor, governor, tanks, brake chambers, slack adjusters, and service/parking/emergency systems. Operating Procedures: proper checks, monitoring gauges, low pressure response, and brake lag. Inspection and Maintenance: inspecting components, recognizing issues, and reporting problems.

Test Structure

Written multiple-choice exam. Most jurisdictions require at least 80% to pass. Some states include a practical component.

Common Mistakes

Confusing service brakes with emergency or parking brakes. Failing to understand pressure levels. Not practicing the system check.

Preparation Tips

Use official CDL study guides. Watch instructional videos. Attend training. Take notes and quiz yourself regularly.

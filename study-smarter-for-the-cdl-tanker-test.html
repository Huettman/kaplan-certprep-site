How to Prep for SHRM Cp

Jul 28, 2025 · 6 min read

Introduction

Preparing for the SHRM-CP exam can be a significant milestone in your HR career. Whether you are looking to boost credentials, increase earning potential, or take the next professional step, passing requires a strategic approach that fits your busy schedule.

Understand the Exam Structure

The test consists of 160 questions divided into knowledge-based and situational judgment items with a four-hour time limit. It covers four key knowledge areas: People, Organization, Workplace, and Behavioral Competencies. Familiarize yourself with the SHRM Body of Applied Skills and Knowledge.

Develop a Personalized Study Plan

Create a plan early in your prep process. Set realistic weekly goals including dedicated study hours blocked on your calendar, target dates for completing each content area, and time for review and practice exams. Breaking study into manageable chunks reduces stress and improves retention.

Choose the Right Resources

Select study tools that align with your learning style and schedule. Effective resources include online prep courses with flexible access, practice exams and quizzes, flashcards and mobile apps for on-the-go review, and study guides and SHRM-approved texts.

Use Practice Questions

Practice questions help you become familiar with the exam format and improve time management. Simulated exams give you a realistic view of your readiness. Review both correct and incorrect answers. Understanding why an answer is right or wrong reinforces learning.

Join a Study Group

Study groups on LinkedIn, Facebook, or professional HR forums provide motivation, accountability, and different perspectives. Many prep courses include access to a learner community.

Time Management and Self-Care

Schedule regular breaks. Maintain healthy sleep and nutrition habits. Avoid cramming the night before. Taking care of physical and mental well-being helps you stay focused.

Exam Day

Know the location of your testing center or remote proctoring requirements. Arrive early, bring proper identification, and understand the rules and procedures. Being familiar with the process reduces anxiety.
